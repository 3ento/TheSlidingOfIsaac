• Game

    Cain is a character unlocked by obtaining 55 or more coins at once in a run.

    Cain starts with two Red Heart containers , one Key , and Lucky Foot . He also gets the paperclip trinket after donating 68 coins.

    Lucky Foot changes bad pills into good pills (similar to PHD, although not telling the pill's effect).

    Strategy: Cain's kit makes him the best character for hoarding items. His starting luck increases the chance of a drop after a room clear, and Lucky Foot boosts this even further by increasing his success for room drops and machines like Slot Machines, Fortune Telling Machines, Shell Games, etc.

    As Cain is likely to have a large collection of coins or keys after a few floors, it is wise to open up Shops, bomb Shopkeepers, donate ten coins to a Donation Machine, or donate to a Beggar till he pays out to maximize the chances of an Angel Room. Cain benefits more from them compared to Devil Rooms due to his low health.

    Because Lucky Foot changes bad pills into good pills, pills are extremely effective for Cain. This makes Golden Pills and (in Repentance)Echo Chamber great finds for Cain, as he will receive multiple guaranteed buffs from the pills.

  • Lore

    Cain is a figure mentioned in the Bible, particularly in the book of Genesis. He is known as the firstborn son of Adam and Eve, the first human couple created by God. The story of Cain and Abel, his younger brother, is one of the well-known narratives in the Bible.

    According to the biblical account, Cain and Abel both offered sacrifices to God. God favored Abel's offering, which was a lamb, but rejected Cain's offering of fruits and vegetables. Out of jealousy and anger, Cain lured Abel into a field and killed him. When God questioned Cain about Abel's whereabouts, Cain famously responded, "Am I my brother's keeper?"

    As a consequence of his crime, God cursed Cain, making the land unproductive for him and forcing him to be a wanderer. However, God also placed a mark on Cain to protect him from being killed by others. The story of Cain and Abel is often interpreted as a cautionary tale about jealousy, disobedience, and the consequences of sin.

    Connection with game lore: Isaac often sees himself as a sinner, as seen in many endings, particularly in ending 17. So naturally many of the playable characters are sinners. Abel can actually be encountered ingame as an item , a familiar that mirros Isaacs movement and shoots 3.5dmg tears. When playing as Cain, the damage is upped to 7.5 and the tears turn red.
