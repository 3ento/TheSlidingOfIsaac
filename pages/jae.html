Jacob&Esau

  • Game

    Jacob and Esau are a character pair added in The Binding of Isaac: Repentance. They are unlocked by defeating Mother with any character.

    Jacob and Esau are controlled as one, move at the same speed, and use the same coins, Bombs, and Keys. Otherwise, they are completely independent in terms of stats, items, and health. Both characters have collision and can be separated, though the "Drop" (Ctrl by default) button can be held to keep Esau in place while Jacob is free to move around the room.

    If either Jacob or Esau die, they both die regardless of the other character's remaining health.

  • Lore

    The story of Jacob and Esau is a prominent narrative in the Book of Genesis, specifically in chapters 25-36. Jacob and Esau were twin brothers born to Isaac and Rebekah, and their story is characterized by themes of sibling rivalry, deception, and divine intervention.

    Here is a summary of the key events in the story:

    Birth and Early Life:

    Rebekah, Isaac's wife, was barren, but after praying to the Lord, she became pregnant with twins. The twins struggled within her womb, and God revealed to Rebekah that two nations were in her womb and that the older would serve the younger.

    Esau was born first, and he was described as a skillful hunter, while Jacob was born holding onto Esau's heel, hence the name "Jacob," which means "heel-grabber" or "supplanter."

    The Birthright and Blessing:

    Esau, as the firstborn, held the birthright, which entitled him to a double portion of the family inheritance. Jacob, however, was cunning. On one occasion, Esau returned from hunting famished and sold his birthright to Jacob for a bowl of lentil stew.

    Later in the story, Isaac, who was old and blind, intended to bless Esau before his death. However, with Rebekah's assistance, Jacob deceived Isaac into blessing him instead.

    Jacob's Flight and Reconciliation:

    Fearing Esau's anger, Jacob fled to his uncle Laban's house in Haran. While there, Jacob worked for Laban and married Leah and Rachel, Laban's daughters. After many years, Jacob decided to return to his homeland, and on the journey back, he reconciled with Esau. Esau, despite initial anger, embraced Jacob, and they reconciled.

    Name Change:

    During Jacob's journey, he had a mysterious encounter with a divine being (often interpreted as an angel or God Himself). Jacob wrestled with this being and demanded a blessing. As a result of the encounter, Jacob's name was changed to Israel, meaning "he who strives with God" or "God strives."

    The story of Jacob and Esau is significant in biblical narrative and has theological implications. It is seen as illustrating God's sovereignty in choosing Jacob, the younger twin, over Esau, and it sets the stage for the formation of the twelve tribes of Israel. The descendants of Jacob (Israel) become the Israelites, while the descendants of Esau become the Edomites, and their interactions are depicted in various parts of the Bible.
