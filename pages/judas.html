Judas

  • Game

    Judas is a character unlocked after beating Satan (Sheol) for the first time. Judas starts with one red heart container, and has The Book of Belial upon starting, as well as 3 coins. Judas also has a 1.35x damage multiplier.

    A damage multiplier is a hidden stat that calculates how much damage Isaac starts with. Given the way damage is calculated (see below), Judas' damage multiplier of 1.35 gives him around 75% more damage from dmg ups (ex. Pentagram- Isaac would get +1.69, while Judas gets +2.27)

    Strategy: With his naturally high DPS, Judas' only concern is the low HP. So you'll generally want to play safer until you amass some health. Blood of the Martyr synergizes with his Book of Belial, so be on the lookout for it. Any items that activate on low health, such as Whore of Babylon, Glyph of Balance and Dark Price's Crown.

  • Lore

    In the Bible, Judas refers to Judas Iscariot, one of the twelve disciples of Jesus Christ. Judas is most famously known for betraying Jesus, leading to his arrest and crucifixion. The betrayal took place in the Garden of Gethsemane, where Judas identified Jesus to the authorities by giving him a kiss.

    According to the New Testament accounts in the Gospels (Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John), Judas agreed to betray Jesus for thirty pieces of silver. The exact motivations behind Judas' actions are not explicitly stated in the Bible, and various interpretations exist. Some suggest that he was motivated by greed, while others propose that he may have been disappointed with Jesus' teachings or had other personal reasons.

    Judas' betrayal is a significant event in Christian theology and has been the subject of artistic and literary works throughout history. The term "Judas" is sometimes used metaphorically to refer to someone who betrays another person, particularly a trusted friend or ally.

    Connection with game lore: Isaac finds himself sinful, so he imagines himself as famous sinners. The theme of betryal could be linked to Isaac feeling guilty over his parent's divorce, blaming himself, as if he "betrayed" them and the family as a whole.
