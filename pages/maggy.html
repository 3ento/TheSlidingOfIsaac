Maggy

  • Game

    Magdalene, or Maggy, is a character unlocked by obtaining seven or more heart containers at once in a run.

    Magdalene starts with 4 hearts and 1 pill, guaranteed to be a Full Health pill after completing the April Fools challenge. She starts with Yum Heart by default.

    Strategy: Sacrifice rooms are this character's main draw. She has an advantange with anything that requires health, such as Curse rooms, Blood donation machines, Devil begars. With her quite heart containers, taking Devil Deals is also less punishing, combined with use of Sacrifice rooms, makes for a slow start but often heavily offensive style of play, as the character takes care of defense by default.

    Items that benefit her greatly:

    Bloody Lust - damage taken gives a dmg up for the floor, up to 6 times.

    Habit ,9 Volt, Car Battery - This combo pretty much breaks the character, as Yum Heart takes less damage to charge than it heals. This is mitigated in later floors without Wafer or something similar, but still extremely powerful.

    Candy Heart - permanent stat ups for red hearts taken. Self explanetory.

  • Lore

    Mary Magdalene is a figure mentioned in the New Testament of the Bible. She is often identified as a follower of Jesus Christ. The Gospels describe her as a woman who had been healed of seven demons by Jesus and who subsequently became one of his close companions.

    Mary Magdalene is particularly prominent in the accounts of the crucifixion and resurrection of Jesus. According to the Gospels, she was present at the crucifixion and, after Jesus' burial, she went to the tomb on the third day to anoint his body. There, she encountered the risen Jesus, and he instructed her to go and tell the disciples about his resurrection.

    Over the centuries, Mary Magdalene has been the subject of various interpretations and traditions. In some Christian traditions, she is considered a symbol of repentance and redemption, while in others, she is regarded as a prominent disciple and witness to the resurrection. However, it's important to note that the Bible does not explicitly state that Mary Magdalene was a repentant sinner or a prostitute, as some later traditions have suggested. These interpretations have been influenced by various cultural and artistic representations over time.

    Connection with game lore: In a cutscene we see Isaac with a wig, with kids laughing at him. From that, Maggy can be interpreted as Isaac's humilation from nonconforming. As for the Bible, the theme of repentance is one found in a few of the game's endings. But considering her early addition, she's likely a reflection of Isaac's desire to be healed of the demons the indoctination makes him think he's full of.
